Emotional Intelligence

9 September 2025

What is Emotional Intelligence?

EQ is the ability to recognise, understand, and control one's own emotions and reactions while also being able to recognise and acknowledge other peoples emotions and feelings. Some people may be good at recognising their own emotions and being able to respond accordingly but not good at recognising or acknowledging other peoples. Having high EQ is being able to do both, and crucially acknowledging feelings i.e. empathising with others, not just recognising them. EQ is also about one's ability to build strong interpersonal relationships.

How is EQ different to IQ?

EQ differs from IQ as it can't be objectively measured in a standardised way the same way that IQ can. IQ assesses a person's logical reasoning and cognitive ability, whereas, EQ is someone's aptitude for recognising and understanding own and others' emotions. Someone may have very high IQ but may lash out others, turn to addiction, or feel disconnected from the world around them - having a higher EQ would mitigate this, particularly in relation to being able to manage one's own emotions.

Why is EQ important?

EQ is important because it is the cornerstone to building healthy and long-lasting relationships with those around them and to feeling connected with yourself. EQ can be developed so having a low EQ isn't the end of the world, but without working on it, lower EQ (paired with higher IQ) can result in strained relationships, poor job performance, and low mental and physical health due to the inability to manage your own emotions and inability to empathise with others. In the work place, a higher EQ is essential for team collaboration, giving and receiving constructive feedback, and as a leader for building a healthy work culture and environment.
