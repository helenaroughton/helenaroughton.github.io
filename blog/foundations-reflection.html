Foundations Reflection

2 October 2025

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

  1. I have learnt that the way other people who know me and love me see me confirms how I generally see myself but also is an enhanced version of how I see myself.
  2. I have learnt that I like the practical side of human skills the most - i.e. I like learning about the techniques to think like a programmer, I like the idea of displaying ability to grow confidence (rather than just believing in yourself to grow confidence), I liked the focus on hauora, and I like the fact that if I prioritise playing strategy based games this can only benefit me and increase my neuroplasticity.
  3. I learnt that I struggle with prioritising mindfulness even if I want to try. I tried it twice and faced an obstacle with the app and never went back to it again so I think for the rest of the course I will find another way to incorporate this into my day.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

One of the biggest roles these play would be in team work and collaboration and knowing how to communicate effectively. All three play a big part in how you communicate as a person and how you're able to engage with other people around you. Secondly (but not any less important) is also how you're able to look after yourself and hauora. I think as an addition, they also drive why you want to become a programmer and eventually the kind of learning and programming you want to do.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

I think what surprised me the most is feeling mixed about some of the human skills learning. I am supportive of human skills learning as really see the benefit of learning and understanding yourself and others etc. I really enjoyed parts of it but I think there are aspects for me where I felt it was too basic and didn't add anything new for me on what I already do as a person i.e. good listening/being aware of emotions and others etc. Learning about the neuroplasticity and growth mindset was really great as this went beyond and what I got the most from.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

Learning and completing exercises on things I know/do when it took time away from other things.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

It is important! No matter how good a person may be technically, if they can't work with others, it's going to be hard in the work place, and it's so important to be able to display you can collaborate with others as all jobs involve this. I think in technical jobs it can also be easy to get bogged down in details and forget to see the bigger picture so it's important to be able to recognise this and also be receptive/good at communicating if someone else recognises this.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

Despite my answers to some of the above questions, no I don't feel it was a waste of time. It's so important, it was a reprieve at times, I think the reflections were insanely beneficial, and I really like practical stuff - thinking like a programmer, and the inclusion of developing a learning plan and te whare tapa wha. Some of those last things are probably things I would have liked to do for myself anyway but maybe felt as though I wouldn't have had time so it was amazing to have this as part of the study. At times I definitely would have liked to use the time to do programming instead but overall definitely not a waste.
