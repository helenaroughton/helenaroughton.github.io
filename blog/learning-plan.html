bootcamp learning plan

3 October 2025

Longterm Goals

Ultimately I hope to add something positive to the world and do some good with the skills I gain from Dev Academy. What this looks like specifically I don't know for certain but currently lean towards becoming a software engineer within renewable energy or a health tech company or a technical product owner/manager. My biggest goal and focus is to feel proud of the work I do and know that it contributes to something bigger that aligns with my values in trying to help others and society.

While at Dev Academy I would like to develop my skills in giving and receiving constructive feedback and speaking in front of larger groups as well as being able to represent myself and my ideas with confidence. I think my biggest non-technical challenge will be the pace of the content for 9 weeks and staying on top of it enough that I feel confident enough to speak in front of the group.

Bootcamp

In order to accomplish these goals, I expect to bring my best possible self to bootcamp. I will ensure I am available during the expected hours and that any tasks that have been set to me are completed by the agreed timeframes. In order to meet this, if I get stuck and am struggling, I'll make sure to ask for help from peers or facilitators so that any tasks that people have expected me to complete are finished in time. I think depending on the challenge or issue I have, I'll set myself a 10 min or 30 min timer and if I haven't solved it within that timeframe I'll ask for help. With this in mind I also hope that the facilitators will treat everyone the same with kindness and respect and are able to understand well the question being asked and able to articulate or explain the answer in a way that is easy to understand.

Looking back to my strengths and limitations I noted during sprint 2, I think those held up pretty true and haven't changed too much. I did notice that what I thought would be the most distracting (messages/emails) wasn't as bad as I thought - the pomodorro technique has really helped me with this and in focusing as I tell myself it's just 25 minutes. I surprised myself that even amongst high stress levels with a lot going on, that when I sat down to complete study I was able to focus entirely on what needed to be done which is a strength I hadn't realised as much before.

I'm hoping these ideas and strengths will carry through to bootcamp and so anticipate my strengths going forwards to be my motivation and ability to cope under stress. I think my biggest limitations will be not prioritising my wellbeing plan as I have noticed particularly, that exercise and other hauora activities are the first to go out the window when I am stressed and have a lot on. I also noticed, however, that when I gave myself proper rest breaks, including outside time, my brain worked better, and I fixed/understood things better so I'll also ensure I prioritise this so my cognitive function is at its best possible.

foundations learning plan

8 September 2025

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

While I'm still figuring some things out, I know I ultimately would love to end up working as a software engineer for a renewable energy company or a health tech company. I love to problem solve and for a long time I've aspired to do something good for the world and contribute to making the world a better place. I hope to build things to make this happen.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning?

I'm curious and I tend to pick things up quickly and a lot of this comes from wanting to know the why behind what I'm learning. I think wanting to know the why is a good motivator for learning, however, sometimes I've noticed I need to trust the process and if it feels as though there is a piece of information missing, the gap is generally filled in the following task or week.

I definitely get easily distracted by messages/emails, admin to organise, and other people. This has been particularly hard these last two weeks as amongst studying, I'm relocating back to NZ in the last week of Foundations, and I anticipate my stress/distraction levels increasing. I am also determined and organised, and I hope these strengths will override any moments of distraction.

What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Confidence levels and patience could do with a boost! I get easily demolarised if I don't understand something right away and it becomes super easy to compare yourself to others but it's always good to remind yourself that everyone is on their own journey.

Collaboration & Workload

During foundations, I plan to front-load my week so that when I need help, there is more time to get help from facilitators while being in a different time-zone, and also to be able to participate/collaborate with others in my homegroup better. For bootcamp, I will have moved back to NZ to complete this, and so this course is my number one priority and commitment. I also understand other people within learning and teaching environments will have different commitments and things on, so communication with others is key.

I have also liked the use of the toggl timer and pomodoro method to track how long I spend on tasks and to ensure I give myself breaks even when I don't think I need one. As the course ramps up more I will continue to ensure I take good breaks, and step away from the computer, going for a walk always helps me clear my head and I come back able to tackle the task more productively. I will use the foundations help desk channel or private message to seek help if I haven't been able to solve the problem quickly myself. While in different time zones, I will move to the next task until I'm able to get help with the one I'm stuck on.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

Kindness, respect, and patience when we come to them for help and when they're helping us with a problem. I hadn't thought too much about expectations but when I think back to times when teachers have been the most helpful it's when they're able to show the three things mentioned above, and a good understanding of the problem and ability to articulate the solution/answer.

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things.

During week 6 of foundations I'll be travelling from the UK to NZ. My flight leaves London, Monday 29th September 10pm (UK time), and I arrive in Nelson, Wednesday 1 October 10pm (NZ time) so will be largely unavailable those days. At some stage, I'll be living with my brother, his partner, and daughter who is 2.5 years old, so I may occasionally need to look after her, but I don't anticipate this to be super disruptive.
